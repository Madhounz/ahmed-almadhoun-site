title: A Framework for Evaluating Freight Transport Poverty Using Accessibility
  Indicators
venue: Submitted to TRANSBALTICA 2026
abstract: "This paper proposes a conceptual framework for evaluating transport
  poverty in freight transportation through accessibility-based indicators.
  While transport poverty is commonly discussed in relation to passenger
  mobility, freight systems also shape economic connectivity by affecting the
  ability of businesses, regions, and communities to access logistics services
  and supply networks. The study defines freight transport poverty through three
  main dimensions: freight network accessibility, logistics service availability
  and reliability, and economic accessibility. The proposed framework provides a
  basis for identifying areas where infrastructure limitations, poor
  connectivity, or service gaps create hidden disadvantages in freight movement.
  By linking accessibility indicators with freight system performance, the paper
  contributes to a broader understanding of transport poverty beyond passenger
  transport and supports more evidence-based infrastructure and logistics
  planning."
